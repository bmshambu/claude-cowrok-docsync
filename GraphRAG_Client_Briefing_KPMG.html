KPMG03
Baseline · How most AI document tools work

Traditional RAG finds text that looks similar

It chops documents into chunks, turns each into a vector of numbers, and returns the chunks whose wording most resembles your question. It works when the answer sits in one passage — and struggles when it doesn't.

Strong when…

the answer lives in a single passage

  • "Summarise the scope of this one RFP"
  • "What does the RFP say about timelines?"
  • Quick retrieval of a stated fact

Weak when…

the answer must be counted, filtered or assembled

  • "How many RFPs exceed USD 2M?" — can't reliably count
  • "Which RFPs require X?" — misses ones worded differently
  • "Compare across all 100" — sees only the top few chunks
  • No concept of relationships between facts
KPMG04
Our Approach · How GraphRAG works

GraphRAG stores the facts and follows the connections

Every buyer, service, budget and driver is a node; every "requires" or "has budget" is a link. Answering becomes filtering and following links — not guessing from similar text.

1
Find the entity — match your question to a node, e.g. Energy Holdings or the ERP Implementation service.
2
Follow its links — hop along relationships such as requires and has_budget to gather connected facts.
3
Zoom out when needed — read 10 community summaries for "across all RFPs" questions instead of re-reading 100 files.
4
Answer with citations — every fact traces back to a document, page and section.
